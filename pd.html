Personal Development Page

Twelve Pillars by Jim Rohn and Chris Widener

The story starts off with a guy who is forty years old, married to his wife
of 15 years that he no longer is very connected with, and two kids who he barely
sees. And a boring job where he makes about 40K as a sales rep.
This story holds many values that can be used in everyday life.


Pillar #4:The major reason for setting a goal is for what it make of you to accomplish it

"Goal setting is deciding where you want to go and then designing a plan to get you there.
Setting goals that stretch you and make you do things differently is what will lead you to a
future that you want. Don’t wait for people to set goals for you."

This resonates with me because I've sometimes, like almost anybody in life have felt stuck
doing the same routine perpetually and grown tiresome of it. Setting goals for myself helps
for me to naturally give myself a purpose instead of just existing and floating like a kite
in the wind not knowing to where the it will take me.

Whethere it be as miniscule as doing 10 pushups in the morning or going on runs every once
in a while, it brings me closer and closer to whatever my end goal might be.


Pillar #5: Everyday has many opportunities, but only one best opportunity.

"We all must suffer from one of two pains: the pain of discipline or the pain of regret.
The difference is that discipline weighs ounces and regret weights tons. Your best
opportunity each day are those that align with your overall goals and should be what
you spend your time on. Don’t just go through the day, get from the day. Don’t spend you time on urgent,
spend your time on what is important. We must be our own designer of our lives."

For me, I completely agree with this quote, to make the most out of my time throughout the day,
it shoud be occupied by finding ways to accomplish my goals or breaking what I thought my limit is.
Overall becoming better by doing what I truly want. The feeling of regret is worse than the feeling
of discipline. My understanding what discipline is from the text is eliminating all distractions
and focusing on the process itself and enjoying the journey to its fullest knowing that what I'm doing
is in fact fulfilling.


Pillar #6: Don’t join an easy crowd; you wont grow. Go where the expectations and the demands to perform are high.

"Ask yourself these questions: Who am I around? What effect are they having on me? What have they got me reading?
What have they got me saying? Where do they have me going? What do they have me thinking? And most importantly,
what do they have me becoming? Then ask yourself the final question: is that okay?"

If I surround myself with those who's goals and values arent in alignment with mine then I'll always be influenced
or distracted from what I'm doing. Answering those questions makes you realize how much valkue certain people in
your life have. Ive heard this many times,"Show me your friends and Ill tell you who you are.", and it is a true
statement due to the fact that if you are surrounded by those who waste their time, you will also waste your time,
likewise if you surround yourself with productive individuals, you yourself will be productive.


Pillar #7: Formal education will make you a living. Self education will make you a fortune.

Self-education is what you teach yourself. It is what you learn along the way so that you are constantly
improving and growing. Most people don’t continue to learn and grow, to separate yourself from everyone else,
be focused on continuous learning.

I've always been somebody who is curious and whatever it is that peaks my interest, I'll find a way get to it
or get information about it. From reading books made for my own personal development, my knowledge of self exceeds
that of some of my peers. People say you can't teach an old dog new tricks but I believe no matter what age I may be
that itch of curiosity will stay with me until I get to omy death bed. Not even just books, it may be even traveling
and learning about a new culture. There is so much knowledge just at our finger tips so why should I not let my
curiosity take over?


I can truly say that overall this book has many lessons that can be in corporated into anyones day-to-day
